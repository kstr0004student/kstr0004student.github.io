The Shroom Hypocrisy

You wish you'd spent money on a bigger outdoor space. You live in a unit on the outer suburbs of Sydney, so there isn't much room for you and your love of plants. Today you feel like a risotto, so you've ventured out into the shoebox sized terrarium you call a garden to pick some mushrooms. The door hinges squeal as the flywire shuts behind you. You've got your mushroom digging gloves on; you're excited. You bend down to press your fingers into the soil and wriggle out the oyster mushroom.
It comes out with a squelch, and you flip it over in your palm
It doesn't budge, the roots hold on for dear life
